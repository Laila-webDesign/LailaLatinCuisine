Pernil (Shredded Pork Shoulder)

Pernil (pehr-NEEL). Pernil is a slow-roasted pork shoulder typically made in an oven. The crispy skin you see on the top left is known as the cuerdo which is often cooked seperately three-quarters of the way through the process. The cuerdo is crispy, crunchy, and salty in a way which makes it inseperable from the pernil and of the meal as a whole. Pernil is a savory and tender meat one can simply not live without.

What to know before you cook:
  • Prep time: 45 minutes (plus an additional 24 hours for marination)
  • Cook time: 4 Hours
  • Serving size: 1/2 pound to 1 pound (or 2-3 people per pound)
  • Servings:10-15 people
  • Allergens: wheat, soybeal oil (depending on sofrito ingredients)
Ingredients: Steps:
  • Pork Shoulder (pernil) approx. 8-10 lbs
  • Distilled White Vinegar
  • Lime (one for cleaning and one for garnish)
  • 1 Tbsp Garlic Powder
  • 1 Tbsp Dominican Oregano (or any oregano)
  • 1 Tbsp Salt (plus an additional tbsp or two for the skin)
  • 1 Tbsp Black Pepper
  • 1 Tbsp Adobo
  • 1 Tbsp Paprika
  • 1 Spoonful of Sofrito
  • 1/4 cup Olive Oil
  1. Remove pernil from package and place on a cutting board
  2. Use a sharp knife to seperate the skin from the pernil (but not fully) and then to poke holes all over the meat
  3. Place the pernil in a large bowl in the sink and pour white vinegar, lime juice, and a bit of water over the pernil. Then move solution over the pernil to clean the meat.
  4. Rinse the pernil with water and put to the side
  5. In a pilón or a seperate small dish, add in about 7 colves of garlic and orgenago. Smash to make a paste
  6. In the same dish add in seasonings and oilve oil. Mix
  7. Pull back the pernil skin and then pour and spread the marinade over the meat making sure the it gets into the holes you made earlier
  8. Place the pernil in a baking dish with the skin back on top of the meat
  9. Wipe excess marinade off the top of the skin with a paper towl and pat dry to remove any additonal moisture
  10. Pour salt over the skin
  11. Cover baking dish with aluminum foil
  12. Place pernil in fridge overnight
  13. Pre-heat oven to 330°F
  14. Peel back the aluminum foil and pat dry the skin with a paper towel
  15. Place pernil in oven for 3 hours
  16. After 3 hours, remove the aluminum foil and raise oven temperature to 450°F and bake for 1 hour
  17. Once done, remove the skin from the pernil and shred meat and place in a seperate dish
  18. You can choose to cut the skin with scissors or with a knife and to place it on top of the shreded meat. Cut a lime and place on pernil for garnish.
  19. Serve and enjoy!
