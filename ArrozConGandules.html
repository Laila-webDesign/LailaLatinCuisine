Arroz con Gandules (Rice with Green Piegon Peas)

Arroz con Gandules is known to be the national dish of Puerto Rico. If you ever get to try this soft and flavorful delicacy, you will find that each Puerto Rican home has their own recipie. The traditional way to prepare this dish is to simmer the oil, add in seasoning with meat of choice, then add in water or chicken broth, and finally add in the rice and cook. This traditional way of preparing arroz con gandules can often be tricky and hard to prepare. In this recipie, I offer you insight into my family's way of cooking arroz con gandules which is simple, easy, and delicious!

What to know before you cook:
  • Prep Time: 5 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 Minutes
  • Serving size: 1/2 cup of cooked rice
  • Servings:10 people
  • Allergens: wheat, soy, MSG, legumes
Ingredients: Steps:
  • 5 cups of water
  • 4 cups Medium Grain Rice
  • 1 Can of Gandules (Green Piegon Peas)
  • Bannana Leaf
  • Aluminum Foil
  • 2 Tbsp of Olive Oil
  • 3-4 Bay leafs
  • Adobo*
  • Salt*
  • 2 packets of Sazon
  • Garlic Powder*
  • Onion powder*
  1. Measure and pour 5 cups of water into your pot
  2. Take off the skin of the chorizos and cut into small pieces
  3. Add olive oil, seasonings, and chorizos
  4. Set flame to high, cover with a lid leaving a small gap so water doesn’t spill, and let water boil
  5. In a medium bowl, measure 4 cups of rice and rinse with water to clean rice
  6. Once water is boiling, add in 4 cups of rice and mix so water covers rice
  7. Lower flame to medium/low. Cover top of pot with aluminum foil, put a lid on the pot, and let cook for 20 minutes
  8. Once the water is all gone, lower flame to low.
  9. Drain can of gandules. Mix in your can of gandules and form a mountain
  10. Add the banana leaf of top of mountain and let cook for an additional 10 minutes
  11. Once the timer goes off, gently mix rice
  12. Serve and Enjoy!

Notes:

*All seasonings are to taste
